Pangasinan is a coastal province in the Philippines located in the Ilocos Region of Luzon. It is the eighth largest cultural-linguistic group of the Philippines, with a population of approximately 3.16 million. The province is crescent-shaped and occupies 5,368.82 square kilometers of verdant farmlands, hills, forests, and rivers. To the east, it is bounded by the mighty Cordillera Mountains, the Zambales ranges to the west, the rice plains of Tarlac to the south and the Lingayen Gulf and the China Sea to the north. The Pangasinan people are predominantly Roman Catholic.
